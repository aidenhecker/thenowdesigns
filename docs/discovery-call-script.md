# The Doctor's-Approach Discovery Call

**The premise (Studio Bible §14):** a good doctor doesn't pitch you a prescription the second you sit down — they ask where it hurts, then *listen*. Your job on this call is to diagnose, not sell. The person who talks least, wins. Aim to talk ~30% of the time. **No price on this call** — price goes in the proposal, last (§14).

**Before the call:** glance at their current site + Instagram for 3 minutes so you can reference something specific. Have the intake-form link ready to send after.

---

## Stage 1 — Rapport (2–3 min)

Warm, human, unhurried. Comment on something real (their work, their city, the thing that warmed the lead). You're a person they'd enjoy working with, not a vendor running a script.

> “Hey [Name], good to actually talk. I had a look at [their thing] — [genuine specific compliment]. How's [the launch / the season / the new spot] going?”

Let them answer. Match their energy.

---

## Stage 2 — The pivot: “Okay, so…” (smile)

This is the gear-change. Smile when you say it — they can hear it.

> “Okay, so— rather than me talk *at* you, I'd love to just understand what you're working with. Mind if I ask you a few things and mostly listen?”

They'll say yes. You've just earned permission to run the call.

---

## Stage 3 — The four-point prompt, then SHUT UP (~10 min)

Ask these four, one at a time. After each, **stop talking.** Silence is the tool. Let them fill it. Take notes — visible note-taking makes people feel heard and makes them say more.

**1. You.** “Tell me about you — how'd you end up doing [their business]?”

**2. The business.** “And the business itself — who are your customers, and how do they find you right now?”

**3. What you want from the site.** “When the new site is live and working perfectly — what is it *doing* for you? What changes?” *(This is the gold. It's where the real value — and your price — comes from.)*

**4. How you see me fitting in.** “And how do you picture working with me on it — what would make this easy and good for you?”

**Listening prompts** (use sparingly, only to keep them going): *“Say more about that.”* / *“What's the cost of that staying the way it is?”* / *“If that one thing worked, what's it worth to the business?”* Then go quiet again.

> Note for you: when they answer #3 with something like “I'd book 5 more jobs a month” and a job is worth $800 — that's $4,000/mo of value. *That* is what the site is worth, not what it costs you to build. Write the number down. It sets your band.

---

## Stage 4 — Play back what you heard (1–2 min)

Reflect it so they feel understood and you confirm the brief:

> “So if I've got this right: you've got [strength], but [the problem] is costing you [the stakes], and a site that [the outcome they named] would mean [their words back to them]. That about it?”

When they say “yeah, exactly” — you've won the project. The proposal is now a formality.

---

## Stage 5 — Deflect the curveballs (don't get cornered)

They *will* ask “so how much?” or “can you do X?” mid-call. Do not quote on the spot — you'll lowball or scare them. Deflect cleanly and warmly:

> **On price:** “Great question — I don't want to pull a number out of the air. Let me put real, exact numbers in a short written proposal after this so you can see what you're getting. I'll have it to you within [24–48 hrs].”

> **On a feature you're unsure about:** “Good one — let me check the cleanest way to do that and confirm it in the proposal rather than guess now.”

Never “I don't know.” Always “I'll confirm that for you.” It reads as diligence, not uncertainty.

---

## Stage 6 — The close: name the next step (1 min)

Don't ask “do you want to work together?” Assume the next step and make it effortless:

> “Here's what I'll do: I'll send you a short intake form — takes five minutes, just so I've got your details and assets in one place — and a written proposal with exact pricing by [day]. You read it, no pressure, and we go from there. Sound good?”

Then: send the intake form link (thenowdesigns.com/client-intake-form.html) and the proposal within 24–48h. **Return the ball fast — money loves speed.**

---

## The one-page cheat sheet (keep this on screen during the call)

1. Rapport — be a person.
2. “Okay, so…” (smile) — get permission to ask.
3. Four points: **you / the business / what you want from the site / how you see me fitting in.** Then shut up ~10 min.
4. Play it back until they say “exactly.”
5. Deflect price + curveballs to the proposal.
6. Close on the next step: intake form + proposal by [day].

**Talk less. Smile. Write down what the outcome is worth. Price later.**
