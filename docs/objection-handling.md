# Objection Handling — The Now Designs

**Posture:** every objection is a request for reassurance, not a no. Stay warm, never defensive, never discount on reflex. Reframe to value (Studio Bible §14). If you don't know, “I'll confirm that for you” — never “I don't know.”

---

**“It's more than I expected / can you do it cheaper?”**
> “Totally fair. The honest version: a typical agency charges $10,000–$50,000 for a custom site — most of that is overhead, account managers, and office rent you'd be funding. I run a handful of clients at a time with no middlemen, so you're paying for the build and nothing else. I won't drop the number, but I can scope it down — we can start with the pages that earn their keep and add the rest later. What's the one page that has to be right?”

**“Why not just do it myself on Squarespace/Wix?”**
> “You absolutely could, and for some businesses that's the right call. The difference is what happens after: those load slower, look like everyone else, and quietly cost you customers who bounce. I hand-code yours so it loads before they blink, ranks from line one, and is genuinely yours. You're buying back the 40 hours you'd spend fighting a template — and a result that actually converts.”

**“What's the difference between the $99/mo and the one-time?”**
> “Simple. $99/mo + $900 to start is the low-upfront way — I build, host, and maintain it, and you stay flexible month to month. The one-time build is yours outright forever: you own the code, domain, and data, and after that it's just optional hosting. Monthly if you want it handled; one-time if you want to own it. Based on what you told me, I'd lean [X] for you — here's why.”

**“How do I know it'll be good — you don't have reviews yet?”**
> “Straight answer: I'm early, and I'm not going to pretend otherwise. That's exactly why you get my founding rate and my full attention — your project isn't one of fifty. You can see the concept builds on the work page to judge the craft, you get two full rounds of changes so nothing ships you're not happy with, and you don't pay the back half until it's done and you love it. The risk is mostly on me, not you.”

**“The neuro / VRIBE thing — is that real?”**
> “Fair to push on. To be clear, it's not mind-reading or a per-visitor service — it's a design *review* grounded in published attention research: how people read and react to a page in the first few seconds. I apply that thinking to every layout so the work is informed, not guesswork. It's a sharper way of designing, not a science claim I'm selling you.” *(Note: if the live copy still says “in-silico brain-response model,” soften it per the copy audit before this question gets asked.)*

**“Can you do it faster?”**
> “Usually yes — most go live in 4–6 weeks, and if you've got your copy and photos ready we move faster. The thing that sets the speed is you getting me the last 20% — your words, your images. Get those to me quickly and I'll match your pace.”

**“What if I want changes after launch?”**
> “Built in. Every project includes two full rounds of changes before launch and 30 days of support after. Past that, the Care Plan ($150–250/mo) covers ongoing edits and hosting — or, because you own the code, any developer can pick it up. No lock-in either way.”

**“Can I pay over time?”**
> “We start with 50% to reserve your build slot and the other 50% when it's done and live — so you're never paying for something you haven't seen. If you need to spread it further, tell me your situation and I'll see what I can do. I'd rather make it work than lose a good project over timing.”

**“I need to think about it.”**
> “Of course — it's a real decision. Can I ask what specifically you want to chew on? If it's price, scope, or timing, I can probably clear it up right now. And just so you know, I take a limited number of builds at a time, so if you're leaning yes, the deposit holds your slot — no commitment to start until you're ready.”

**“Do you do online stores?”**
> “For real selling, I'll point you to a dedicated commerce platform — it's the right tool and I won't pretend otherwise. What I'd build you is the site around it: the brand, the pages that sell, the experience that makes people trust you before they hit checkout. Want me to scope that?”

---
**Golden rule:** never negotiate against yourself. Reframe to value once, offer to *re-scope* (not discount), and let the 50/50 deposit + “you own everything” + “you don't pay the back half till you love it” carry the risk off the client's shoulders.
