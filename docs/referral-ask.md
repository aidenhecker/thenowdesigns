# The Referral Ask — The Now Designs

**Why this is your cheapest growth (Studio Bible §14):** a happy client is your best salesperson, and ~75% of web work moves through proximity, not the open market. Being the easiest, most pleasant designer they've worked with is what makes them *want* to introduce you. Referrals are warmer than any DM you'll send.

**The two rules:** (1) ask at the moment of maximum happiness — right after launch or after a specific win. (2) Make a *specific* ask, never “know anyone who needs a site?” — a vague ask gets a vague nothing. Name a category or a single person.

---

## Timing

Ask at one of these peaks, not before:
- **At handover**, when they first see the live site and they're delighted.
- **2–4 weeks post-launch**, when they message you with a result (“we got 3 bookings this week!”) — that's your cue.
- **The moment they leave a testimonial** — attach the referral ask to it.

---

## Script A — Text / DM (at handover or after a win)

> So glad you love it — honestly made my week. Quick favor: I take on a handful of clients at a time and I grow almost entirely by word of mouth. **Who's one person you know whose website doesn't do them justice?** A [their industry] owner, someone who just opened up, anyone. A one-line intro from you is worth more than anything I could do on my own — and I'll look after them the same way I looked after you.

## Script B — Email (slightly fuller, after a result)

**Subject:** A small ask (and thank you)

> Hi [Name],
>
> Seeing [the result they reported] genuinely made my week — that's exactly what the site was built to do.
>
> One small ask. I keep my roster small and I grow by referral, not ads. If one person comes to mind — someone whose website is holding their business back — would you be open to a quick intro? Even just “you two should talk” and my email is plenty.
>
> To make it easy, here's something forwardable:
>
> *“I worked with Aiden at The Now Designs — he hand-built our site, it's fast, it's ours to keep, and he was genuinely easy to work with. If your site needs sorting, talk to him: aiden@thenowdesigns.com.”*
>
> No pressure at all — you've already been great to work with.
>
> Aiden

---

## The forwardable blurb (give them the words — don't make them write)

> *“If your website needs sorting, talk to Aiden at The Now Designs. He hand-builds fast sites, you own everything, and he's the easiest designer I've worked with. aiden@thenowdesigns.com / @thenowdesigns.”*

---

## Optional: a referral incentive (introduce once you have ~3 clients)

Make it a clean, two-sided thank-you so it feels generous, not transactional:

> “And if an intro turns into a project — I'll knock **$250 off your next year of hosting/care** (or send it your way as a thank-you), and give them a founding-friendly rate too. Win for both of you.”

Keep the number simple and tied to something you already provide (Care Plan credit costs you little, means a lot). Never make the incentive the headline — the relationship is the headline; the credit is the cherry on top.

---

## After they refer someone

- **Thank them within the hour**, specifically. Referrals stop when they feel unnoticed.
- **Treat the referred person like gold** and report back to the referrer (“thank you for [Name] — we had a great call”). Closing the loop is what earns you the *second* referral.
- Log every referral source in your sheet so you know who your real advocates are — those are the people to over-deliver for forever.
