# The Now Designs — Warm-Outreach Playbook

**Rule zero (Studio Bible §14):** the platform is the niche — never niche by client type. Chase people who *already want a site*. ~75% of web work never hits the open market; it moves through proximity, not skill. If your gut says “I'd have to convince them,” that's a funnel leak — skip them and find a warmer name. You're not selling. You're opening a conversation that ends with one easy yes: *a free call.*

---

## 1. Build the Warm Pond (do this first — 30 minutes, target 40 names)

List people in concentric rings, hottest first. Put them in a simple sheet: `Name · Ring · Warm signal · Channel · Last touch`.

**Ring 1 — Already asked / already hinted.** Anyone who has said “I need a website” or “mine's embarrassing.” These are not leads, they're appointments you haven't booked yet. Message today.

**Ring 2 — People you actually know with a business or side-hustle.** Friends, family, old coworkers, classmates, the person who cuts your hair, your trainer, your dentist, anyone freelancing or running a shop. You have standing to message them with zero awkwardness.

**Ring 3 — Businesses you're a real customer of.** The café you go to, the gym you train at, the mechanic you trust. You can honestly say “I'm a regular and I noticed…” — that's a real relationship, not a cold pitch.

**Ring 4 — Trigger-event businesses.** Warm because *timing* makes them want a site right now: just rebranded, opened a second location, hiring (= growing), running paid ads to a slow/ugly site, posted “new menu/services,” or got a new owner. The trigger is your reason for reaching out.

**Ring 5 — Communities you're genuinely in.** Alumni groups, local Facebook/Discord groups, sports leagues, faith communities, niche forums. Warm because there's a shared context to lead with.

**The filter for every name:** *Would I be comfortable walking up and saying this to their face?* If no, they're cold — cut them.

## 2. Instagram DM scripts (your primary channel — @thenowdesigns)

Keep it short, specific, about THEM, and end on a soft, low-commitment ask. No price, no pitch, no portfolio dump in message one.

### Variant A — Someone you know (Ring 1–2)
> Hey [Name] — random one. I've started a little web studio (The Now Designs), building fast, hand-made sites for people I'd actually want to work with. Are you still running [their thing]? If you ever want a second set of eyes on the site — totally free, no pitch — I'd genuinely love to. Either way, hope you're good!

### Variant B — Local business you're a customer of (Ring 3)
> Hi [Business] — I'm a regular (the [oat latte / Tuesday class / whatever] is the best in town). Quick honest one: I build websites for a living and yours is the only thing that doesn't match how good the [coffee/space/work] is. I'd be happy to walk you through what I'd change on a free 15-min call — no strings. Worth a chat?

### Variant C — Trigger event (Ring 4)
> Hey [Name] — saw you just [opened the new spot / rebranded / launched the new menu], congrats! That's usually the exact moment the website starts feeling a step behind everything else. I run a small studio that builds these fast (live in weeks, you own everything). Want me to send over what a refresh would look like? Free call, zero pressure.

### Variant D — Referral-warmed (someone vouched for you)
> Hi [Name] — [Mutual] mentioned you might be looking to sort out your website soon. I build hand-made, fast sites — and I take on only a handful at a time so each one gets real attention. Happy to do a free call and just tell you what I'd do and what it'd cost. When's good?

**DM rules:** one ask per message. Never paste a price. If they reply with interest, your *only* goal is to book the call — “Amazing — easiest thing is a quick 15-min call so I can actually look at it with you. Does [day] or [day] work?” Send the intake link only after the call.

## 4. Follow-up cadence (where most deals are actually won)

Money loves speed (§14): **return every ball within 24 hours.** Be the easiest, most pleasant person they've talked to — that's what turns a client into a salesperson later.

- **No reply after 3 days:** one soft bump. *“No worries if the timing's off — leaving the door open. Free call's there whenever.”*
- **No reply after 7 more days:** value-add, then stop. *“Last one from me — here's a 30-second thing you can fix on your current site today: [one genuine tip]. If you ever want the full version done for you, you know where I am.”*
- **Two unanswered bumps = done.** Move the name to a “check back in 90 days” list. No chasing. Warm ponds refill.

**Weekly target for the first $10K:** 10 new warm DMs/emails out + every reply answered same-day. At a realistic 15–20% reply-to-call rate, that's 1–2 calls a week — enough to book the 3 founding builds.
